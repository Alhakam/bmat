Cement designationdp back to ToC or Data Property ToC

IRI: https://w3id.org/bmat#cementDesignation

Cements according to DIN EN 197-1 are marked by the following information: - the main type of cement and the standard designation, - the abbreviation of the cement type, - the strength class 32.5, 42.5 or 52.5 - the initial strength: N (usual initial strength), R (high initial strength) or L (low initial strength), valid only for CEM III cements and - a number or two freely selectable lower-case letters in brackets, provided that a manufacturer produces different cements with the same standard designation in a single plant in order to distinguish them from each other (see example). Normal cements with low hydration heat are additionally marked LH, normal cements with high sulphate resistance SR. For CEM I with high sulphate resistance, the C3A content in the clinker must also be indicated.

Concrete can be classified according to its compressive strength. Powdery or liquid additives which influence certain properties of the concrete. Exposition class of concretedp back to ToC or Data Property ToC

IRI: https://w3id.org/bmat#expositionClassConcrete

In order to ensure sufficient durability, concrete components must be sufficiently resistant to chemical and physical influences from their environment and use. The impacts from the various environmental conditions are classified in exposure classes which can act on the concrete, the reinforcing steel or metallic components and which are not considered as loads in the structural design. This results in the requirements for the composition of the concrete to be used, as well as the concrete covering and the permissible crack width. The designations of the individual exposure classes are composed of the letter X (for exposure), the identification for the type of damaging influence and a number indicating the intensity of the damaging influences. The following abbreviations are used for the different types of actions: 0 for Zero Risk C for carbonation D for Deicing Salt S for Seawater F for frost A for Chemical Attack M for Mechanical Abrasion
has super-properties
concrete attribute dp
